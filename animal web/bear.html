BEAR

A bear is a large, stocky mammal with thick fur, a short tail, and non-retractable claws. They have a strong sense of smell, short, rounded ears, and a long snout. There are eight species of bears found across North America, South America, and Eurasia, with diets ranging from primarily carnivorous to herbivorous and omnivorous depending on the species. Physical appearance: Bears have a large, heavy body with stocky legs and plantigrade paws. Their fur is often shaggy, and they have short, stumpy tails. The length and weight can vary greatly by species, with polar bears being among the largest. Sensory abilities: They possess a powerful sense of smell but generally have poor eyesight. Anatomy: A bear's snout is pointed, and they have short, rounded ears and small eyes. A brown/grizzly bear has a distinctive shoulder hump and a "dished in" face profile. Habitat and diet: Bears live on the continents of North America, South America, and Eurasia. Their diet varies; some, like the polar bear, are primarily carnivorous, while others, like the brown bear, are omnivorous. Species: There are eight different species of bears, including the brown bear, polar bear, and panda bear.
