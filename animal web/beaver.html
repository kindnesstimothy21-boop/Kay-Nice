BEAVER

A beaver is a large, semi-aquatic rodent known for its thick, brown fur, large incisor teeth, and a broad, flat, scaly tail. They have short legs, webbed hind feet for swimming, and dexterous front paws for manipulating objects. Beavers are known for being vegetarian, nocturnal, and for their ability to build dams and lodges using sticks, mud, and rocks. Physical characteristics Size: Large and stout, they can be up to five feet long including the tail and weigh up to 90 pounds. Fur: Dense and waterproof, it is typically brown but can range from yellowish-brown to black, with a paler underside. Tail: A broad, flat, paddle-shaped tail covered in scales, used as a rudder in the water or to slap the surface as a warning signal. Feet: Large, webbed hind feet for swimming and smaller front paws that are used like hands. Head: Small eyes and ears, and large, chisel-like, orange incisors for gnawing trees. Behavior and habitat Habitat: Semi-aquatic, they live in ponds, rivers, and streams. Activity: Nocturnal and active year-round, they do not hibernate. Diet: Vegetarian, they eat only plants, not fish. Social Structure: They mate for life and live in family groups called colonies. Communication: They make a variety of sounds and slap their tails on the water to signal danger. Territory: They mark their territory with a musky secretion called castoreum.
