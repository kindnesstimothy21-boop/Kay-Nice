HORSE

A horse is a large, strong, hoofed mammal with a long face, a mane, and a tail. They have four legs ending in a single toe, or hoof, and a muscular, deep-bodied build. Horses are herbivores and are known for their speed and strength, having been domesticated by humans for thousands of years for transportation, work, and sport. Physical description Size: A typical domestic horse stands about 5 feet tall at the shoulder and weighs approximately 1,150 pounds, though breeds vary significantly in size. Body: They have a muscular and deep torso, long and slender legs, and a large head. Limbs: Each leg ends in a single toe encased in a hard covering called a hoof, made of the same material as a human fingernail. Mane and tail: They are covered in short hair, but have a mane of longer hair along the top of their neck and a long tail made of coarse or silky hair. Color: Horses come in many colors, including brown, black, white, and grey, or a mix of these. Characteristics and abilities Speed: They are built for speed and have four gaits: walk, trot, canter, and gallop. Strength: Their legs are strong, with a muscular upper part and a lower part that acts like a spring. Senses: They have a nearly 360-degree field of vision and excellent hearing. Intelligence: Horses are highly intelligent and can be trained for a wide variety of tasks. Sleep: They are one of the few animals that can sleep standing up. Relationship with humans Domestication: Humans have domesticated horses for over 6,000 years, using them as a primary mode of transportation and for heavy labor before the invention of cars. Modern uses: Today, horses are used in police work, a variety of equestrian sports like racing and dressage, and as a hobby.
