LION

A lion is a large, muscular cat with a tawny coat and a long tail tipped with a dark tuft. Male lions are distinguished by a thick mane around their head and neck, which can be light to very dark and varies by individual. Lions are social animals that live in groups called prides and are known for their powerful roar. Appearance Build: They have a muscular, deep-chested body, large heads, and strong jaws. Coat: Their fur is short and ranges in color from yellow-gold to light brown or grayish. Cubs have faint spots that disappear as they grow. Male vs. Female: Males are larger and have a mane that can be full, shaggy, or simply fringe the face, varying in color and size based on age and genetics. Females (lionesses) lack manes and are smaller and more agile. Tail: Both sexes have a tuft of dark hair on the end of their long tail. Behavior and social structure Social groups: Lions are the only cats that live in family groups called prides, which can include up to 40 individuals. Hunting: Lionesses typically do most of the hunting, using teamwork to stalk and ambush prey like zebras, wildebeests, and antelope. Territorial defense: Male lions primarily defend the pride's territory and protect it from other predators and rival males. Communication: They communicate through roars that can be heard up to five miles away, which helps them warn intruders and stay in contact with other pride members. Other facts Habitat: They primarily live in grasslands, scrub, and open woodlands in Africa, with a small population also found in India. Physical adaptations: Lions have retractable claws and a rough tongue to help them grasp and peel prey. They also have loose skin on their belly to protect them from kicks. Diet: Their diet consists mainly of large mammals, but they will also eat smaller animals and scavenge when necessary. Activity: They are most active at night and spend about 16 to 20 hours of the day resting or sleeping.
