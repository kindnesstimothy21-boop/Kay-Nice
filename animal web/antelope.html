ANTELOPE

Antelopes are graceful, hoofed mammals known for their slender builds, long legs, and speed. They are herbivores in the Bovidae family, which also includes goats, sheep, and cattle, and are primarily found in Africa and Asia. All antelopes have permanent horns with a bone core covered in keratin, though horn shape and size vary greatly among species. Physical characteristics Body: Slender, agile bodies with long legs and slim necks. Horns: Permanent horns that grow throughout their lives. They are made of a bone core covered in keratin, and their shape varies from straight to spiral or curved. Coat: Most have short, dense fur in earthy shades like tan, reddish-brown, or gray, often with pale underbellies. Some species have striking markings or stripes. Size: A huge variety in size, from the tiny royal antelope to the massive giant eland. Senses: Acute senses of smell and hearing, which help them detect predators. Behavior and diet Diet: Herbivores that are also ruminants, meaning they chew their cud. Movement: Known for their speed and agility, with some species exhibiting incredible leaps. Social structure: Varies by species, with some living solitarily and others forming large herds. Defense: Use their speed and agility to escape predators. Some species have white rumps that serve as a warning signal to others when they run. Habitat and distribution Primary habitat: Found mainly in Africa, which is home to 72 native species, and Asia. Diversity: They are a very diverse group, with species adapted to a wide range of habitats, including grasslands, forests, and deserts.
