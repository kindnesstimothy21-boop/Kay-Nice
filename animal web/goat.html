GOAT

A goat is an agile, sure-footed ruminant mammal known for its horns, beard (in males), and for being a domesticated animal raised for milk, meat, and wool. Goats are intelligent and curious social animals, with males called bucks or billies, females called does or nannies, and their young called kids. They digest food using a four-chambered stomach and are characterized by a light build and short tail. Physical characteristics Horns: Typically have horns that arch backward, though horns vary in shape and size by breed and sex. Build: Lighter of build compared to sheep, with straight hair and a short tail. Beard: Male goats often have a beard. Stomach: Are ruminants, meaning they have a four-chambered stomach (rumen, reticulum, omasum, and abomasum) and chew their cud. Feet: Are even-toed ungulates. Behavior and characteristics Social: Intelligent, social, and curious animals that are more independent than sheep. Diet: They are herbivores that will eat a wide variety of plants, and are known for their ability to clear weeds. Communication: Goats may use a sneeze sound to warn the herd of potential danger. Water: Dislike water and prefer to jump over puddles or streams rather than walk through them. Domestic and wild varieties Domestic: Capra hircus are domesticated breeds raised for milk, meat, and wool. Wild: Wild goats include species like the ibex and markhor.
